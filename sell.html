Not Sure Which to Do?

Selling vs. Pawning Your Item

Both get you cash today. The difference is whether you want the option to get your item back.

✔ Selling Your Item

  • You get more cash upfront — selling pays more than pawning the same item
  • ✅ Transaction is permanent and final — no coming back for it
  • ✅ No fee, no redemption date, nothing to track
  • ✅ Best if you're ready to part with the item
  • ✅ Same-day cash, same process

🔄 Pawning Your Item

  • 🔑 You keep the right to get your item back by paying the pawn fee
  • ✅ No credit check — no debt created at all
  • ✅ If you don't redeem, you owe nothing — walk away free
  • ✅ Best for items with sentimental value or that you'll want back
  • ✅ Same-day cash, same process

Get the Most for Your Item

Tips to Maximize Your Offer

01

Clean It Up Before You Come In

A clean, well-presented item signals it was cared for — and that shows in the offer. This is especially true for tools, electronics, and firearms. You don't need to restore it, just clean it.

02

Bring Everything That Came With It

Original boxes, cases, chargers, extra magazines, certificates of authenticity, documentation — all of it helps. A firearm with its original case gets a better offer than the same gun without one.

03

Know the Make and Model

The more specific you are, the faster and more accurate the offer. "Glock 19 Gen 5 with night sights" gets a better result than "a black handgun." Look up your item before you come in.

04

Call Before You Make the Trip
