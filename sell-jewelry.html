Know Before You Come In

What Affects Your Jewelry Offer

  • 💎

    Diamond Quality (The 4 Cs)

    Cut, color, clarity, and carat weight all affect diamond value. A GIA or AGS certificate helps — but we evaluate diamonds without paperwork too. We'll tell you what we see.

  • 🥇

    Metal Karat & Weight

    The gold or platinum setting has its own value separate from the stones. Higher karat gold and heavier settings mean a better overall offer.

  • 🏷️

    Brand or Designer

    Tiffany, Cartier, Bulgari, and other designer pieces carry a premium beyond the metal and stone value. Brand matters — bring any paperwork or boxes you have.

  • 📊

    Current Resale Market

    Jewelry values fluctuate with gold prices, diamond demand, and trends. We check current market conditions before making any offer.

  • 📄

    Certificates & Paperwork

    GIA certificates, original receipts, and appraisals help — but they're not required. Bring what you have; we evaluate the piece regardless.

💡 Tips to Get the Best Jewelry Offer

Bring any paperwork. GIA certificates, original boxes, purchase receipts, and prior appraisals all help us verify what you have and support a stronger offer.

Don't clean it first. We prefer to evaluate jewelry in its current state. Cleaning can sometimes damage settings or make it harder to see the condition we're working with.

Bring the whole set. Matching sets — ring, earrings, necklace — are worth more together than sold separately.

Pawn if you're unsure. If you're not ready to permanently sell, pawning lets you get cash now and get the piece back later. No legal debt, no collections.
