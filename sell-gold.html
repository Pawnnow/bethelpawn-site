How We Price Your Gold

We don't guess and we don't hide how offers are calculated. Here's exactly what happens when you bring gold or jewelry to Bethel Pawn.

1

We Test the Purity

Using an acid test or electronic tester on-site, we verify the karat purity of your gold — 10K, 14K, 18K, etc. This takes about 60 seconds and you watch the whole thing.

2

We Weigh the Gold

Your item is weighed on a precision scale in grams. If there are non-gold components (stones, clasps, etc.) those are weighed separately and not included in the gold calculation.

3

We Apply the Live Spot Price

We pull the current spot price of gold in real time. Your offer is based on actual gold market value — not an arbitrary percentage set weeks ago.

4

We Show You the Math

You see exactly how the offer was calculated — weight × purity × spot price. If your jewelry has diamonds or valuable stones, those are assessed separately and added to the offer.

5

You Decide — Zero Pressure

Accept the offer and walk out with cash, or take your item and walk out with nothing owed. We never pressure you and we never hold your item during the appraisal.

Higher karat = more gold content = higher offer per gram. A 14K ring weighing 5 grams contains more gold than a 10K ring of the same weight.

Not sure what karat your jewelry is? Most pieces are stamped inside the band or on the clasp — look for "14K", "585", "750", or similar markings. If you can't find it or aren't sure, bring it in and we'll test it for free.

What Condition Does Bethel Pawn Accept?

Many people don't bring in their gold because they think condition matters more than it does. Here's the truth.

✔ We Buy This

Broken & Damaged

Broken chains, bent rings, cracked settings, missing stones — none of it affects the gold content. We buy scrap gold by weight and purity. A broken 14K chain is worth the same as an intact one of the same weight.

✔ We Buy This

Old & Outdated Styles

Jewelry you never wear, inherited pieces you don't want, outdated styles from the 80s or 90s — all fine. We're buying the metal and stones, not the design. Age and style are irrelevant to the gold value.

✔ Always Worth Bringing In

Single Earrings & Incomplete Sets

Lost one earring from a pair? Single gold earrings, incomplete sets, and orphaned pieces all have full melt value. Don't throw them away — bring them in.

💡 Tips to Get the Best Offer

Sort by karat before you come in. Group your 14K pieces separately from 10K and 18K — it speeds up the appraisal and helps you understand the value of each group.

Don't clean it with chemicals. A gentle rinse is fine, but avoid harsh jewelry cleaners before bringing it in — they can affect test results.

Bring everything at once. Combining multiple pieces into one transaction is easier for everyone, and seeing the full picture lets us give you the most accurate assessment.

Not sure if it's real gold? Bring it anyway — we test it for free. You'll know within minutes whether it's 14K solid gold or gold-filled. No charge either way.
