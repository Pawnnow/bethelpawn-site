Know the Difference

A Pawn Is Not a Loan

This distinction matters — for your rights, your credit, and your peace of mind. Here's exactly how a pawn differs from any loan product.

✔ A Pawn — What Bethel Pawn Offers

Pawning Your Item

  • ✅ You leave an item as collateral and receive cash
  • ✅ No legal debt is created — ever
  • ✅ You choose whether to redeem your item
  • ✅ If you walk away, you owe us nothing
  • ✅ Zero impact on your credit score
  • ✅ No collections, no legal action possible
  • ✅ No credit check required
  • ✅ The item — not you — satisfies the transaction
✗ A Loan — What a Pawn Is Not

A Traditional Loan

  • ❌ Creates a legal debt obligation
  • ❌ You are personally liable to repay
  • ❌ Non-payment leads to collections
  • ❌ Can damage your credit score
  • ❌ Can result in legal action against you
  • ❌ Subject to consumer lending regulations
  • ❌ Often requires credit check or application
  • ❌ You remain liable even after the term ends

When you pawn at Bethel Pawn: you are never a borrower. You are never in debt. Your credit is never at risk. The item is the transaction — you are not.

Under Ohio law, pawn terms run in 30-day periods. To redeem your item, you pay back the pawn value plus the applicable fees — Ohio sets a 6% finance charge per 30-day period plus a $6.00 storage fee per period. We explain the exact redemption amount and date clearly at the time of your transaction — no surprises. You can redeem early at any time at no penalty. To extend, simply pay the fees before your redemption date and your pawn resets for another 30 days. Call us at (513) 427-4705 before your date and we'll always work with you.

Ohio Pawn Fee Example

Pawn value: $100  →  6% finance charge = $6.00 + $6.00 storage = $12.00 to redeem after 30 days. Extend for another 30 days by paying the same $12.00 fee before your due date. These are the state-set maximums — always confirmed in writing at the time of your transaction.
