How We Price Your Gun

What Determines Your Gun's Offer

  • 📖

    Blue Book Value

    We reference Blue Book of Gun Values and current dealer pricing for your specific make, model, and caliber. This is the industry standard for firearms pricing.

  • 📊

    Current Resale Market

    We check what your specific firearm is actually selling for right now — not what it sold for two years ago. Some guns have surged in value, others have softened.

  • 🔍

    Condition

    We grade condition honestly — Excellent, Very Good, Good, or Fair. A gun with original finish and no wear commands a meaningfully better offer than one that's been carried hard for 10 years.

  • 📦

    Original Box, Papers & Accessories

    Original box, extra magazines, night sights, optics, or a quality holster all add to the offer. A gun in the original box with all papers is worth more than the same gun without.

  • 🔢

    Serial Number & Legal Status

    The firearm must have an intact serial number and clear legal status. We cannot buy guns with defaced or altered serial numbers under any circumstances.

💡 Tips to Get the Best Offer on Your Gun

Know the make, model, and caliber. "I have a Glock 19 Gen 5 in 9mm" gets a faster, better offer than "I have a black pistol." Look at the slide, barrel, or grip for stampings.

Bring the original box and accessories. Original box, extra magazines, any optics or aftermarket sights — all of it adds up. Even if the box is worn, bring it.

Clean it before you come in. A clean, well-maintained firearm signals care and gets a better offer than a gun caked in powder residue or neglect.

Bring it unloaded. Ohio law and common sense — bring your firearm unloaded when coming in to sell or pawn it. Transport it in a case if possible.

Ohio & Federal Law

What You Need to Sell a Gun in Ohio

Selling or pawning a firearm in Ohio is straightforward. Here's exactly what's required before you come in.

🪪

Valid Photo ID

A valid government-issued photo ID is required for all firearm transactions. Ohio driver's license, state ID, or U.S. passport. Your ID must be current and unexpired.

📅

Age Requirements

You must be 21 years or older to sell a handgun. You must be 18 years or older to sell a long gun (rifle or shotgun). Same requirements apply for pawning a firearm.

✅

Legal Ownership

You must be the legal owner of the firearm. We cannot purchase stolen firearms or guns with altered or defaced serial numbers. All transactions are reported per ATF requirements.

ATF Compliance: Bethel Pawn conducts all firearm purchases in full compliance with federal ATF regulations and Ohio law. All transactions are recorded in our bound book as required. We do not purchase firearms from prohibited persons. Selling a firearm to a federally licensed dealer like Bethel Pawn is a legal, straightforward process — we handle all required paperwork on-site.
