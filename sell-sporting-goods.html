Know Before You Come In

What Affects Your Offer

  • 🏷️

    Brand Drives Value

    A Hoyt compound bow and a no-name big-box bow are night and day in resale value. Quality brands like Hoyt, Mathews, Vortex, Leupold, and Shimano hold value well. Off-brand gear is very hard to resell.

  • ✅

    Working Condition

    Gear needs to be in functional, usable condition. A compound bow with a broken string or cam issues, a scope with fogged glass, or a reel with a stripped gear are significantly harder to buy.

  • 📦

    Original Case & Accessories

    Original hard cases for bows and scopes, extra accessories, and original paperwork all add to the offer. A compound bow with its case, release, and sight is worth more than the bare bow.

  • 📊

    Current Resale Demand

    Sporting goods are seasonal — a deer hunting setup in September is an easier buy than in April. That said, we buy year-round and quality gear always has a market. We check current prices before every offer.

💡 Tips to Get the Best Offer on Your Gear

Clean and inspect it first. Wipe down scopes, clean reel drag systems, check bow strings and cams for wear. Gear that's been maintained and shows it gets a better first impression and a better offer.

Bring all the accessories. The original case, extra arrows, bow releases, wrist straps, optic covers, rod bags, and tackle boxes all add up. Don't leave accessories home thinking they won't matter.

Know the model. "Hoyt Carbon RX-7 Ultra" gets a much faster, more accurate estimate than "a nice compound bow." Look at the sticker on the riser or the reel foot for model info.

Call first for seasonal or bulky items. Large items like treadmills or kayaks — call (513) 427-4705 before loading up your truck so we can confirm we're interested and have space at 119 W. Plane St, Bethel, OH.
