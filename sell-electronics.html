Know Before You Come In

What Affects Your Electronics Offer

  • ⚙️

    It Must Work

    We test every electronic item before buying. Power it on, check that it functions normally, confirm there are no major issues. Non-working electronics have minimal resale value and are very difficult to buy.

  • 📱

    Screen Condition

    A cracked screen significantly reduces an offer on phones and tablets. A fully functional device with a cracked screen may still be buyable but expect a lower number. No cracks is always better.

  • 🔓

    Unlocked vs Locked

    For phones and tablets — carrier-locked or iCloud/Google-locked devices have almost no resale value because they can't be used. Unlocked or factory reset devices get full market offers.

  • 📦

    Cables, Chargers & Accessories

    Original chargers, cables, controllers, and cases add to the offer. A PS5 with two controllers is worth more than a PS5 alone. Bring everything you have.

  • 📊

    Current Market Demand

    We check real-time market prices — eBay sold listings and current street value — before making every offer. Electronics depreciate fast, so current pricing matters more than what you paid.

💡 Tips to Get the Best Electronics Offer

Factory reset your device before you come in. This removes your personal data and shows the buyer it's clean and ready for resale. It also removes any account locks that could reduce your offer.

Charge it up. Bring the device with enough battery to demonstrate it works and show us the display and basic functions. A dead device we can't test gets a conservative offer.

Know the model and storage size. A 256GB iPhone 15 Pro and a 128GB iPhone 15 Pro are meaningfully different in value. Check Settings for exact model and storage before you come in.

Bring the controllers and cables. Gaming consoles especially — a PS5 with one controller gets a lower offer than the same console with two controllers and all cables.

Protect Your Privacy

How to Wipe Your Device Before Selling

Always factory reset your device before selling it. Here's how to do it on the most common devices — it takes less than 5 minutes.

  • 1

    iPhone / iPad (iOS)

    Go to Settings → General → Transfer or Reset iPhone → Erase All Content and Settings. Sign out of iCloud first — this removes the activation lock so the next owner can use it.

  • 2

    Android Phone / Tablet

    Go to Settings → General Management (or System) → Reset → Factory Data Reset. Remove your Google account first under Settings → Accounts to avoid Factory Reset Protection lock.

  • 3

    Windows Laptop

    Go to Settings → System → Recovery → Reset this PC → Remove everything. Choose "Local reinstall" — this wipes your data and reinstalls Windows cleanly.

  • 4

    MacBook (macOS)

    Sign out of iCloud first (System Settings → Apple ID → Sign Out), then go to System Settings → General → Transfer or Reset → Erase All Content and Settings.

  • 5

    PlayStation / Xbox

    PS5: Settings → System → System Software → Reset Options → Reset Your Console. Xbox: Settings → System → Console Info → Reset Console → Reset and Remove Everything.

Why This Matters

For your privacy: A factory reset removes all your personal data — photos, passwords, accounts, messages — before the device leaves your hands. This is always the right thing to do before selling any device.

For your offer: A device that's been properly reset and signed out of accounts is unlocked and ready to resell. An iCloud-locked iPhone or Google-locked Android has almost no resale value because the next owner can't use it. Resetting first means a better offer for you.
