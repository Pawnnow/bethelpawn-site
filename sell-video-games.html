What We Look At

What Affects Your Offer

  • 🎮

    Console or Game?

    Consoles generally command stronger offers than individual game discs. A working PS5 with controller is a very different conversation than a single sports title. Complete bundles always do better.

  • 📦

    Complete vs. Loose

    For retro games, complete-in-box with manual is significantly more valuable than a loose cartridge. For consoles, having all original cables and controllers matters. Bring everything you have.

  • ✅

    Working Condition

    We test everything before making an offer. A console that boots up and plays games is worth far more than one that doesn't. Controllers should respond, discs should read, cartridges should load.

  • 📈

    Current Resale Market

    Retro game values fluctuate based on collector demand. Certain titles — even games that looked ordinary at the time — carry serious premiums today. We stay current on the market.

  • 🕹️

    Title & Rarity

    A common pack-in game vs. a limited print run title is a massive difference. Rare N64 titles, specific SNES RPGs, and low-print PlayStation games can be worth real money. Don't assume.

  • 📋

    Collection Size

    Larger collections are always worth bringing in all at once. A 100-game lot gets a different evaluation than a single title. Call ahead if you have a large collection so we're ready.

💡 Retro Gaming Tip

If you have a box full of old cartridges from a parent or grandparent's house — please bring them in before assuming they're worthless. Many people have valuable games sitting in storage that they have no idea about.

Certain NES, SNES, Genesis, and N64 titles that were common rentals in the 90s now sell for $50–$300+. A copy of Stadium Events or Earthbound in good condition is worth hundreds. We know what to look for.

Call us at (513) 427-4705 and describe what you have — we'll tell you honestly if it's worth making the trip.
